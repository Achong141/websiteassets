          What started as designing three planes orthogonal to each other eventually led to the creation of a "cube-house". From there, the concept was developed further into a pavillion for a small wedding site located on the edge of Lake Erie. A key component of my design for the pavillion was to make it so that only the extended plane from within the cube could provide a unique viewing experience towards the lake. I made it so that the site had hedges tall enough to close off the entirety of the lake. Moreover, the site itself had the hedges organized such that each space was separate from each other, and the paths that the attendees would take would lead them through an alternating series of private and public spaces.
